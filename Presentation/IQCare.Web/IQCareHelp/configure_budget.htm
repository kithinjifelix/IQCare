Program and Calendar Year

Definitions:

•Calendar year: a 12 month period from January 1 to December 31 of a particular year.

•Program year: a 12 month period starting when the program year starts and ending after 12 months.

 

Program budgets and funds received do not always line up with a calendar year. For example many of the US government funded programs start October 1st of one year and goes through to September 31st of the following calendar year. Attention has been paid to the fact that program years are not necessary the same as calendar years. When a program is configured, you must enter the "fiscal year start month" which specifies the first month of the program year. This drives other time dependent displays related to programs. See Configuring the master program list for where the fiscal year start month is entered.

 

The calendar year display is used when cross program data is displayed, such as the total budget per month report.

 

In this example Port Reitz has a fiscal year starting in July.

 

Funded Budgets

Some or all of the the budget grid will be disabled if donor funding does not match the budget dates. For example, if a program is funded by a donor for the first 6 months of 2012 (January to June) and you want to enter budget numbers of this time period - the grid cells for July to December 2012. Only funded budgets can be entered. See configuring Donor Program linking for linking programs with donor funding.

 

Enter Budget

First, take note of the fiscal year of the program and the funding dates of the donor.  Port Reitz's fiscal year starts in July (see above); they are funded by the Ministries of Health from January 1 to December 31, 2013.

 

1.From the main navigation select Configure Budget

 

 

2.Select the Donor/Payer, Program and Program Year and the appropriate budget grid displays. Note that text in black means that cells are enabled and you can fill in a budget; if the text is in blue then the cells are disabled and you cannot enter budget numbers.

 

In this example, Port Reitz has a fiscal year from July to June, so the budget grid reflects this fiscal year. The program funding is set from Jan 2013 to December 2013, so months in 2014 are not funded and the budget cells cannot be filled in.

 

 

3.Enter the budget numbers into each cell; you can use the tab key to move from left to right.

4.When all of the numbers have been entered, click Save
