Cost Component Overview - How are costs in IQCare calculated?

 

Costs are split up into four cost allocations categories and costs are calculated based on the clinical services, drugs dispensed and laboratory tests done.

 

Cost Category

Cost Calculation Basis

Estimated/Exact

Human Resources

HR Cost per patient visit = Monthly HR budget and patient visit rate

Estimated

Overhead

Overhead Cost per patient visit = Monthly overhead budget and patient visit rate

Estimated

Pharmacy

Based on the cost of each pharmaceutical and the quantity of drugs dispensed per patient

Exact

Laboratory

Based on the cost of each lab test per patient

Exact

 

 

For each patient visit, if a clinical form is filled out, the cost of the resources and overhead is added to the patient visit cost. If drugs are dispensed, the cost of the drugs are added to the patient visit cost. And finally, if labs are done, the cost of labs are added to the cost of the patient visit. Together, these costs are enumerated to create a debit note that shows a line list of all of the accumulated costs for a patient visit. Depending on the clinic, some of these costs may be covered by one or more donor programs and the patient is not responsible for paying some or all of the costs.

 

To configure cost categories covered by a program:

•See Program Item Linking for how to configure drugs and labs costs

•See Configure Budget and Configure Patient Visit for how to configure salary and overhead costs

 

 

Can I add another Cost Allocation?

IQCare allows you to add another cost allocation category in the Cost Allocation Category master list.

 

How do new/additional Cost Allocations get handled in IQCare?

Any new cost categories that are beyond the 4 predefined categories are not used in calculating patient costs in the debit note.
