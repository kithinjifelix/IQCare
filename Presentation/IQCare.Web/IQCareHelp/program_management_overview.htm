Program Management Overview

  

IQCare provides decision support tools for the program manager with patient management, budget forecasting and cost tracking. IQCare uses Activity Based Costing to calculate the costs of clinical consultations, drug dispensing and lab services.

 

Program Management Cost Flow

 

 

Benefits of providing program management and patient level costs

With detailed cost data that shows how costs are being broken down by cost category and related to the number of patients served program managers can:

•Improve budgeting for each program area and over time

•Identify variations in expenditures to help prioritize areas that need extra funding and areas that need to be scaled down.

•More easily articulate the value of a program to stakeholders and partners

•Definitively show the magnitude of in-kind value of drugs and services

•Engage stakeholders and partners in detailed conversations about costs, expenditures and program accountability

•Increase program funds management effectiveness by clearly highlighting areas where money is being spent

 

Activity Based Costing

The Program Management module uses the concept of activity based costing to calculate the cost of a patient visit. The use or expenditure of a service or a good is multiplied by the cost of that service or good and then added up to make up the total cost of a visit.

 

For example: suppose a patient came into a clinic for an Cervical Cancer follow up visit.

•The cost of the receptionist, clinician, nurse, etc makes up a consultation fee;

•The cost of running the clinic makes up the overhead fee;

•The cost of the drugs dispensed makes up the pharmacy fee

•The cost of the lab tests done makes up the lab fee.
